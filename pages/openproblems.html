  • Group Theory
    • Let $G$ be a group and $H$ and $K$ be finite subgroups of $G$. Then
      $\displaystyle\frac{\mathrm{cent}_K(H)}{\mathrm{cent}_H(K)}\in\left[\frac{1}{2},2\right]$,
      where $\mathrm{cent}_Y(X)$ denotes the number of relative centralizers of elements of $X$ in $Y$. Moreover, the set of all possible values $\mathrm{cent}_K(H)/\mathrm{cent}_H(K)$ is dense in $[\frac{1}{2},2]$ and cover all but a few classes of rationals.
      M. Farrokhi D. G. and F. Saeedi, Finite groups with a given number of relative centralizers, Comm. Algebra 46(1) (2018), 378-385.
    • The minimum number $n$ for which a group $G$ satisfies an identity
      $\displaystyle x^{g_1}\cdots x^{g_n}=1$
      for some elements $g_1,\ldots,g_n\in G$ is called the generalized exponent of $G$, and it is denoted by $\mathrm{gexp}(G)$. Then
      • the exponent of every finite $p$-group with generalized exponent $p$ is bounded above by $p^2$.
      • the generalized exponent of every non-abelian finite simple group is equal to its exponent.
      A. Abdollahi, D. Bounabi, M. Farrokhi D. G., and Y. Guerboussa, Groups of prime generalized exponent, Internat. J. Algebra Comput. 27(7) (2017), 849-862.
    • Is there an infinite group $G$ with non-trivial torsion-free center such that $Z(\mathrm{Aut}(G))$ is an infinite cyclic group?
      M. Farrokhi D. G. and M. R. R. Moghaddam, On the centre of the automorphism group of a group, Bull. Austral. Math. Soc. 92 (2015), 390-396.
    • For a group $G$, let $\mathcal{P}(G)$ denote the power graph of $G$, that is, a graph with vertex set $G$ such that two vertices are adjacent if one is a power of the other. Let $\mathcal{P}^*(G)$ be the proper power graph of $G$ obtained from $\mathcal{P}(G)$ by removing the identity element. Then there exists a constant $c$ such that $\mathrm{diam}(\mathcal{P}^*(G))\leq c$ for all finite groups $G$ with connected proper power graph.
      A. Doostabadi and M. Farrokhi D. G., On the connectivity of proper power graphs of finite groups, Comm. Algebra 43(10) (2015), 4305-4319.
    • Let $V/F$ be a finite dimensional vector space over a finite field $F$. Let $E_1$ and $E_2$ be subfields of $F$. Let
      $\displaystyle\Xi_n(V,F;E_1,E_2):=\sum_{\substack{V=U_1+U_2\\U_1/E_1\leq V/E_1\\U_2/E_2\leq V/E_2}}\left(\frac{|V|}{|U_1|}\cdot\frac{|V|}{|U_2|}\right)^n=\sum_{\substack{V=U_1+U_2\\U_1/E_1\leq V/E_1\\U_2/E_2\leq V/E_2}}\frac{|V|^n}{|U_1\cap U_2|^n}.$
      Compute $\Xi_n(V,F;E_1,E_2)$, in particular for $n=0,1$.
      M. Farrokhi D. G. and F. Saeedi, Subgroup permutability degree of $PSL(2,p^n)$, Glasgow Math. J. 55(3) (2013), 581-590.
    • Let $G$ be a finite group and $H$ be a subgroup of $G$. Let
      $\displaystyle P_N(H,G):=\frac{|\{(h,g)\in H\times G:h^g\in H\}|}{|H||G|}$
      be the normality degree of $H$ in $G$. Also, let
      $\displaystyle\mathcal{P}_N(G):=\{P_N(H,G):H\leq G\}.$
      Then $G$ is solvable if $|\mathcal{P}_N(G)|\leq4$.
      M. Farrokhi D. G., Finite groups with two subgroup normality degrees, Submitted.
      M. Farrokhi D. G. and F. Saeedi, Subgroup normality degrees of finite groups II, J. Algebra Appl. 11(4) (2012), 8 pp.
    • Let $G$ be a finite group and $H$ be a subgroup of $G$. Let
      $\displaystyle d(H,G):=\frac{|\{(h,g)\in H\times G:[h,g]=1\}|}{|H||G|}$
      be the relative commutativity degree of $H$ in $G$. Also, let
      $\displaystyle\mathcal{D}(G):=\{d(H,G):H\leq G\}=\{d_0,d_1,\ldots,d_n\},$
      where $1=d_0>d_1>\cdots>d_n=d(G)$. Then
      • if $H$ and $K$ be two groups of coprime orders, then
        $|\mathcal{D}(H\times K)|=|\mathcal{D}(H)|\times|\mathcal{D}(K)|$.
      • if $|\mathcal{D}(G)|\leq8$, then $G$ is solvable.
      • if $d(H,G)=d_k$, then $\Omega(|H/H\cap Z(G)|)\leq k$. In particular, $\Omega(|G/Z(G)|)\leq|\mathcal{D}(G)|-1$. The result is known to be true for $k\leq3$.
      M. Farrokhi D. G., Finite groups with five relative commutativity dgerees, Results Math. 77(2) (2022), Article 56..
      M. Farrokhi D. G. and H. Safa, Subgroups with large relative subgroup commutativity degree, Quaestiones Mathematicae 40(7) (2017), 973-979.
      A. Erfanian and M. Farrokhi D. G., Finite groups with four relative commutativity degrees, Algebra Colloq. 22(3) (2015), 449-458.
      R. Barzegar, A. Erfanian, and M. Farrokhi D. G., Finite groups with three relative commutativity degrees, Bull. Iranian Math. Soc. 32(2) (2013), 271-280.
    • The probability of being a $2$-Engle group of a finite non-$2$-Engle-group is bounded above by $13/16$, that is,
      $\displaystyle d_2(G):=\frac{|\{(x,y)\in G\times G:[y,x,x]=1\}|}{|G|^2}\leq\frac{13}{16}$
      for all finite non-$2$-Engle-groups $G$. The upper bound is sharp.
      A. Erfanian and M. Farrokhi D. G., On the probability of being a $2$-Engel group, Int. J. Group Theory 2(4) (2013), 31-38.
    • There is no finite group $G$ satisfying the equation
      $K(G):=[G,\mathrm{Aut}(G)]\cong D_{2n}$,
      for all $n\geq3$.
      M. Farrokhi D. G., M. R. R. Moghaddam, and M. Naghshineh, Autocommutator subgroups with cyclic outer automorphism group, Note Mat. 31 (2011), 9-16.
  • Number Theory
    • Let $\mathcal{A}_k$ be the set of all numbers $\frac{1}{n}\left(1+\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\frac{1}{m_i}\right)$ satisfying
      $\displaystyle\frac{1}{k+1}<\frac{1}{n}\left(1+\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\frac{1}{m_i}\right)\leq\frac{1}{k}$
      such that $n,m_1,\ldots,m_{n-1}\geq2$. Then $\mathcal{A}_k$ is contained in finitely many sequences of the form $\left\{\frac{ai+b}{ci+d}\right\}_{i=1}^\infty$. It is known that $\mathcal{A}_1=\{\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{i}\}_{i=2}^\infty$.
      M. Farrokhi D. G. and F. Saeedi, Subgroup normality degrees of finite groups II, J. Algebra Appl. 11(4) (2012), 8 pp.
    • Let the generalized Fibonacci numbers be defined by $U_0(p,q)=0$, $U_1(p,q)=1$, and $U_{n+2}(p,q)=-qU_n(p,q)+pU_{n+1}(p,q)$ for all $n\geq0$, where $p,q$ are real numbers. Also, let
      $\displaystyle{m\atopwithdelims\{\}i}=\left(\prod_{\underset{j\neq i}{j=1}}^mU_{j-i}\right)^{-1}$.
      If $\sum_{i=1}^m{m\atopwithdelims\{\}i}=0$ for some $m$, then
      $$\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{m+n}{m+n\atopwithdelims\{\}i}U_{x_1-n_1i}\cdots U_{x_k-n_ki}=0,$$
      where $n\geq0$, $n_1+\cdots+n_k=n$ and $x_1,\ldots,x_k$ are arbitrary integers.
      M. Farrokhi D. G., Generalization of an identity involving the generalized Fibonacci numbers and its applications, Integers 9 (2009), Article 39, 497-513.
    • Let $p_n$ denote the $n$-th prime number. If $k$ is any integer, then there are infinitly many $n$ such that
      $p_n-2(p_{n-1}-p_{n-2}+\cdots+(-1)^{n-1}p_1)=2k+1$.
      In particular, there are infinitely many $n$ such that
      $p_{2n+1}=2(p_{2n}-p_{2n-1}+\cdots+p_2-p_1)+1$
      and infinitely many $n$ such that
      $p_{2n+1}=2(p_{2n}-p_{2n-1}+\cdots+p_2-p_1)-1$.

      M. Farrokhi D. G., https://oeis.org/A008347.
    • Let $a,b$ be non-zero integers and $f(n)$ denote the maximum prime factor of $an+b$ if $an+b\neq 0$ and $f(n)=0$ if $an+b=0$ for any integer $n$. Then the set
      $\{n,\ f(n),\ f(f(n)),\ \ldots\}$
      is finite of bounded length for any integer $n$.
      M. Farrokhi D. G., https://oeis.org/A006530.
    • If $F_n$ denotes the $n$-th Fibonacci number, then $\sigma(F_n)>2F_n$ if and only if $n$ is a Zumkeller number except for $n=12$.
      M. Farrokhi D. G., https://oeis.org/A074726.
    • A number $n$ has a coprime divisor shift $s$ if $\gcd(d+s,n)=1$ for all divisors $d$ of $n$. The coprime divisor shift of $n$ is the smallest coprime divisor shift of $n$ if $n$ has a coprime divisor shift, and it is infinity otherwise. Then every positive integer $s$ is the coprime divisor shift of a positive integer.
      M. Farrokhi D. G., https://oeis.org/A366219.
    • If $p>7$ is a prime, then there exists a base $b$ such that $p$ in base $b$ is the concatenation of smaller primes in base $b$ (including leading zeros).
      M. Farrokhi D. G., https://oeis.org/A348358.
  • Combinatorics
    • Let $\kappa_n$ denotes the number of permutations $\pi$ of $\{1,\ldots,n\}$ such that $2\pi_1-1,\ldots,2\pi_n-n$ are all distinct. Then
      • $n!\sim n^{1+o(1)}\kappa_n$.
      • $(n-2)\kappa_{n-1}\leq\kappa_n\leq(n-1)\kappa_{n-1}$.
      • The polynomial $\kappa_1+\kappa_2x+\cdots+\kappa_nx^{n-1}$ is irreducible for all $n$.
      M. Farrokhi D. G., https://oeis.org/A322859.
  • Graph Theory
    • A graph is called self $2$-distance if it is isomorphic to its $2$-distance graph. Then there are no regular self $2$-distance graphs of odd degree. The result is known to be true for cubic graphs.
      A. Azimi and M. Farrokhi D. G., Self $2$-distance graphs, Canad. Math. Bull. 60(1) (2017), 26-42.
  • Miscellaneous
    • Let $\mathbf{1}:\mathbb{N}\longrightarrow\mathbb{N}$ count the number of $1$'s in binary expression of $n$. Define $\mathfrak{F}:\mathbb{N}\times\mathbb{N}\longrightarrow\mathbb{N}$ as
      $\mathfrak{F}(m,n)=\min\{\mathbf{1}(xy):\mathbf{1}(x)=m\text{ and }\mathbf{1}(y)=n\}$.
      Determine $\mathfrak{F}(m,n)$ for all $m,n\geq1$.
